What You Can & Cannot Put in a 15 Yard Dumpster

Keep your rental compliant by loading acceptable materials and keeping hazardous waste out. Unsure about a specific item? Call before placing it in the dumpster.

✅ Accepted Materials

  • Household junk: furniture, mattresses, appliances without refrigerants
  • Construction debris: drywall, lumber, flooring, roofing materials
  • Yard waste: branches, leaves, limited dirt or sod
  • Light demolition debris: concrete, bricks, asphalt within weight limits

❌ Restricted Materials

  • Paints, solvents, chemicals, or hazardous waste
  • Electronics: TVs, computers, large appliances
  • Tires, car batteries, and automotive fluids
  • Medical waste, fuel tanks, asbestos, insulation

Need to dispose of restricted materials? We’ll connect you with specialized recycling partners.

Where Can You Place a 15 Yard Dumpster?

This dumpster fits comfortably in most driveways or residential spaces. For street placement, some areas in Philadelphia and South Jersey require a temporary dumpster permit.

Driveway & On-Site Placement

  • Provide at least 60 feet of clearance for the roll-off truck
  • Move vehicles and obstacles from the delivery zone
  • Ensure surface is flat and can support the load (asphalt, concrete, or stabilized gravel)
  • Cover delicate surfaces with plywood for added protection

Street Permits

  • Philadelphia requires a temporary dumpster permit for street placement
  • Many South Jersey municipalities have similar requirements
  • We’ll help you confirm regulations and provide documentation
  • Allow 2–3 business days for permitting when possible

Choosing the Right Size Dumpster

The 15-yard dumpster is ideal for moderate waste volume, but if you’re managing a large-scale renovation, commercial construction, or extensive landscaping, a 20 or 30-yard dumpster may be better suited.

  • Great Fit: Single-room remodels, basement purges, moving prep, or targeted cleanouts.
  • Consider 20 Yard: Whole-home renovations, window replacements, or projects with bulky furniture.
  • Consider 30 Yard: Major construction, disaster cleanup, or commercial build-outs needing maximum capacity.
